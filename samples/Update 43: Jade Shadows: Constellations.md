**New Railjack Missions** 

Pick a side, Tenno. Will you team up with Vena or Ryoku? 

Two new Railjack missions are available to play from the Uranus Proxima after you complete the Jade Shadows: Constellations quest: **Scoria’s Angel** and **The Kuva Wytch**. Take command of Vena or Ryoku’s fully-equipped Railjacks to aid in their objective of breaking into the other’s Capital Ships and dispatching them. 

Each mission is uniquely tied to aiding either Vena or Ryoku in their skirmishes against one another: 

  * **The Kuva Wytch:** Skirmish Against Vena. Aid Ryoku with his Railjack and crew. 
  * **Scoria’s Angel:** Skirmish Against Ryoku. Aid Vena with her Railjack and crew. 



**How to Access** 

You can access the new Railjack missions in the following ways: 

**1. From Navigation** 

  * Enter Navigation and select the Railjack toggle in the top right corner 
  * Select Uranus Proxima 
  * Select The Kuva Wytch or Scoria’s Angel mission node (normal or Steel Path) 
    * More information on Steel Path Railjack in the dedicated section below 



**2. From Pontis Tower** 

  * Enter Navigation and select Uranus (appears in both default and Railjack navigation) 
  * Select the Pontis Tower node 
  * In Pontis Tower walk or fast travel (from pause menu or quick access wheel) to Vena or Ryoku. The fast travel options are: 
    * Skirmish Against Vena (The Kuva Wytch node) 
    * Skirmish Against Ryoku (Scoria’s Angel node) 
  * Proceed through the portal behind Vena and Ryoku to enter their Railjack Dock 
  * Interact with the console to the right to board their Railjack 
    * If matchmaking is set to Solo prior to entering Pontis Tower, you will also see the option to board your personal Railjack. 
  * Select “Begin Mission” from the Navigation console in the Railjack to start the mission 
    * Before beginning the mission, you also have the option to toggle the difficulty between normal and Steel Path. 



**Gameplay** 

Ally with Vena or Ryoku and embark on a treacherous journey in new Railjack missions that combine the Skirmish and Assassinate gamemodes into one. Each mission begins with a Railjack combat sequence to thwart the opposition Protoframe’s operations and gather intel to board their capital ship. 

Once you’ve breached the ship, you and your ally will fight through hazards, traps and special enemy units to go face to face with their rival in a final epic arena battle. Coming out victorious will complete the mission and reward you for your efforts, including special resources for each mission to purchase goods from a secret vendor in Pontis Tower (more on that in the “Rewards” section). 

**Mission Features** 

Both missions have the following features: 

  * **Difficulty:** These missions can be played on Normal or Steel Path difficulty. 
    * We’ve also introduced Steel Path for all Railjack missions! Learn more in the dedicated section. 
  * **Matchmaking:** 
    * **Playing With A Squad:** While playing with a squad (premade and public matchmaking), you use Vena and Ryoku’s special Railjacks, not your own. They are styled to each Protoframe’s aesthetics and come fully equipped with their very own personalized loadouts. 
    * **Playing Solo:** Follow these steps in order to use your personal Railjack: 
      * Set matchmaking to Solo from your Base of Operations 
      * Open Navigation and select Pontis Tower 
      * In Pontis Tower, proceed to the boarding console in Vena/Ryoku’s Railjack Dock 
      * Following the above steps will trigger a secondary boarding option “Board Personal Railjack (Solo)” to appear on the console. Select it to board your personal Railjack. 
      * From inside your Railjack, select the “Begin Mission” option from the Navigation console. 
      * **Important Notes**:   
⠀⠀‣ Any changes you wish to make to your personal Railjack before the mission must be done in the Dry Dock.   
⠀⠀‣ You must follow the above steps in order to use your personal Railjack. Starting the mission Solo from outside of Pontis Tower will default to Vena/Ryoku’s Railjack. 
  * **Crew:** 
    * Playing With A Squad: Vena/Ryoku will join you in their respective missions, as well as their own crew if you do not have a full squad. 
      * Like your own crew, each member can be reassigned a role via the Tactical Menu. 
    * Playing Solo: Your own personal crew will be used instead of Vena and Ryoku’s. 
      * Reminder again that any changes you wish to make to your crew must be done from the Dry Dock before loading into Pontis Tower. 
  * **Railjack Intrinsics:** 
    * Using Vena/Ryoku’s Railjack: Regardless of your personal progress with each Railjack Intrinsics class (Tactical, Piloting, Gunnery, Engineering, Command), when you are playing The Kuva Wytch and Scoria’s Angel missions using Vena/Ryoku’s Railjacks you have access to all the Rank 10 perks. 
    * Using Personal Railjack: Your own Railjack Intrinsics ranks are used in these missions. 
      * Intrinsic points can also be earned during Railjack combat. 
  * **Archwing Disabled:** Since these missions are specifically designed for Railjack and ground combat (once in the capital ships), Archwing is disabled to keep focus on those objectives. 
  * **Unlimited Resource Forge:** This means that players have unlimited Revolite (for Omni Tool), Ordnance Munitions and Dome Charges.

**Swapping Between Sirius and Orion** 

Sirius & Orion allow you to swap between two Warframes. Unlike Equinox, who shifts between her Day and Night forms, Sirius & Orion are separate Warframes with their own builds and abilities. Tap to cast your active Warframe’s ability, or Hold to cast the ability of the inactive Son, thereby swapping to him. 

The inactive Son remains on the battlefield, following the Primary Son and attacking enemies in his wake, similar to Wukong’s Celestial Twin or a Specter. 

Before we get into their kits, we want to go over how equipment and enhancements are shared between Sirius & Orion so you have a better understanding of how they operate. Please note that this list is **not comprehensive**, but rather serves as a general overview of their quirks.

**Individual Enhancements and Equipment:** 

  * Warframe Builds, including Mods* and Arcanes

  * Polarities (including new ones from installed Forma) 
  * Helminth Abilities 
    * Helminth Abilities can only be Infused on the Primary Son. Even if you swap Primary Sons and infuse an ability on both, the Secondary Son will only have their default abilities available in-mission. 
    * Note: Helminth Abilities with a Hold/Tap mechanic will override the Hold to Swap mechanic of the Ability it was subsumed over. So if you Infuse Eclipse over Orion’s first Ability, you’ll be able to use both the damage reduction and damage buff mechanic of that Ability, but won’t be able to swap to Sirius by holding that First Ability anymore. 



**Primary and Secondary Son Mechanic:** 

When entering a mission, you’ll always do so as the Primary Son — either Sirius or Orion. You can swap between brothers easily in-mission but the Primary Son has access to a few special qualities that the Secondary Son does not: 

  * Only the Primary Son can have a Helminth Ability Infused. 
    * Even if you swap Primary Sons and infuse an ability on both, the Secondary Son will only have their default abilities available in-mission. 
  * Only the Primary Son can use items from the Gear Wheel. 
    * Activating an item from the Gear Wheel as the Secondary Son will forcibly swap you to the Primary Son. 
    * **This restriction is considered a bug, not a feature** — we intend to give the Secondary Son the ability to use Gear with our next Cert cycle. 
  * Only the Primary Son will walk around in Hubs and the Base of Operations. 
    * **Known Issue:** Other players will only see Sirius in Hubs even if Orion is selected as the Primary Warframe. 



While these restrictions may seem confusing, think of the Secondary Son as an Exalted Warframe (except maybe don’t tell Sirius or Orion that). The team did their best to give players the most agency when swapping between Sons, but there are ultimately limitations on what we could accomplish. 

The Primary Son changes depending on your progress with the Jade Shadows storyline: 

  * Sirius the default Primary Son if you haven’t completed the Jade Shadows quest* 
  * If you have completed the Jade Shadows quest, the chosen name will become the Primary Son 
  * The Primary Son can be changed at any time in the Pontis Tower (after completing Jade Shadows: Constellations) 



* Since Sirius is the default Primary Son — and therefore Orion is the default Secondary Son (aka Exalted Warframe) — **only one copy of Orion can be owned**. Upgrade and Appearance changes to Orion apply to all Orions if multiple Sirius & Orions are owned.

**NIDUS RETOUCH** 

Nidus burst onto the scene in 2016 as the first Warframe to use an alternate cost for Ability casting. While his Mutation Stacks are a cornerstone of his kit, maintaining them has become more and more difficult as Warframe’s gameplay loop has become faster. Our goals with this retouch are to improve his overall rate of Mutation Stack acquisition, and sprinkle in some general quality of life changes as well! 

**Stat Changes:** 

  * Increased Nidus’ Health from 555 to 675, and Nidus Prime’s Health from 650 to 825. 
  * Doubled his innate health regeneration. 



**Passive** 

  * Increased the maximum Mutation Stacks from 100 to 200. 
  * Improved the Mutation Stack counter’s ability to track rapid Stack growth (as sometimes it would take a while to catch up). 



Abundant Mutation Augment 

  * Increased additional maximum Mutation Stacks from 200 to 300. 



**Ability 1: Virulence** 

Virulence is often cast over and over to quickly build Mutation Stacks. Our aim with the following changes were to make Virulence more impactful when building Mutation Stacks, while hopefully reducing the need to spam-cast. 

  * Virulence now does damage upon initial hit, and then continues to do damage over time for any enemies touched by Virulence during its duration. 
    * This initial damage and the damage over time both contribute towards Mutation Stacks, meaning Virulence won’t have to be spammed to quickly gain Mutation Stacks. 
    * The Energy Refund / Hit still only applies to the initial hit. 
  * Virulence’s duration now scales with Ability Duration. 
    * Also slightly increased the base Duration of Virulence growths. 
  * Improved Virulence’s ability to move across surfaces of varying heights. 
  * Updated Ability Description to specify damage type (Impact) and clarify Damage done per Mutation Stack. 



Teeming Virulence Augment 

  * Increased Primary Weapon Critical Chance from 120% to 150%. 



**Ability 2: Larva** 

Larva is a crowd control tool that synergizes with Virulence, but it was in need of a slight quality of life lift: 

  * Larva’s chance to generate a Mutation Stack now scales with Ability Strength, up to 100% chance. 
  * Larva can now be recast, which will remove the previous Larva in the process. 



Larva Burst Augment 

  * Now applies one guaranteed Toxin Status Effect for every enemy grabbed. 
    * Previously would only apply one Toxin Status Effect to each enemy, but now the number of Effects scales with the number of enemies grabbed. 



**Ability 3: Parasitic Link** 

The most pressing issue to address with Parasitic Link was its ease of use, especially for controller/mobile players. The goal of these changes is to make Parasitic Link more forgiving to cast and maintain. 

  * Improved the targeting on Parasitic Link, as it would often require too much precision for a fast-moving game. 
    * Recasting will also now immediately move the Parasitic Link to the new target. 
    * Players can recast Parasitic Link on the same target to refresh duration. 
  * Parasitic Link no longer costs Mutation Stacks to cast. It now costs 25 Energy. 
  * Added a 3 second grace period before the Link breaks. 
  * Specters and free-moving Companions (like Kavats) can now be targeted. 



Parasitic Vitality Augment 

  * Unchanged. 



**Ability 4: Ravenous** 

Ravenous is a good representation of our technical limitations from 2016 — and the changes below illustrate how far we’ve come since then. This ability benefits the most in this retouch from our work on other Warframe abilities, namely Uriel, and Vauban’s rework! 

  * Ravenous’ radius now scales with Ability Range. 
  * Increased Health Regen per second from 20 to 75. 
    * Healing pulses now also cleanse Status Effects. 
  * Maggot improvements: 
    * Maggots now trigger Summoner’s Wrath. 
    * Improved Maggot targeting logic and overall speed. 
    * Reduced the Maggot spawn delay. 
    * Recasting Ravenous to refresh its duration (while you’re standing on it) will now cause Maggots to explode. 
    * Added damage numbers to Maggots’ attacks. 
  * Fixed newly spawned Maggots not taking Nidus’ updated Mutation Stacks into account for damage scaling. 
    * Previously they would use the Stack count when Ravenous was initially cast. 



Insatiable Augment 

  * Unchanged.

**VRAM Utilization Improvements** 

Historically Warframe was overly conservative with VRAM on Windows and would often leave multiple gigabytes unused on even mid-tier systems. Now, where possible, Warframe will take advantage of unused VRAM on Windows to improve the performance of texture and mesh streaming.
