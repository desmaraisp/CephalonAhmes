**CLAN COOPERATION SYSTEM** 

We mentioned some exciting changes coming to Clans with our [Clan QoL Dev Workshop](https://forums.warframe.com/topic/1495904-dev-workshop-upcoming-clan-quality-of-life-changes-the-shadowgrapher/). We are calling this collection of changes the Clan Cooperation System as it is our intention to incentivize more cooperative play between Clanmates. We are going to get into greater specifics in the section below. 

**Clan Vault Resource Bonus** 

As part of a new incentivized structure to support playing with your Clanmates, this update introduces a new Clan Vault Resource Bonus. By playing with other Clanmates you now generate bonus resources that are deposited into your Clan Vault at the end of your mission. This bonus is **in addition** to whatever you collect, so your personal resources amounts are not affected. The resources eligible for this bonus are only those that can be stored in your Clan Vault. Some of these resources are not currently required for Clan use (for example in Dojo decorations) but may be used in the future! 

The Clan Vault Resource Bonus works as follows: 

  * Each player in a squad from the same Clan increases the bonus % of additional resources that will be deposited into the Clan Vault at the end of the mission. 
    * Two players results in a 10% resource bonus per player 
    * Three players results in a 15% resource bonus per player 
    * A full squad results in an 20% resource bonus per player 
  * The bonus % of additional resources is applied to all eligible resources collected by you and your Clanmates. 
  * All bonus resources are immediately deposited into the Clan Vault upon mission completion. 
  * You can view the resources that your squad has added to your Clan Vault in the end of mission screen. 



We believe these bonus resources in your Clan Vault will make it easier than ever to complete Research, fund Dojo rooms, and decorate your Dojos!

**Quest Flow & Tutorial Changes** 

The following changes were made with the goal of making each moment feel exciting and punchier, while also ensuring we are properly teaching new players the game in a fun and rewarding way. We want players spending less time running around to get to the next waypoint and more time fully immersed in the action as they learn. So we’ve made the following changes to achieve that: 

  * Moved the sprint and jump tutorial popup earlier upon exiting the Orokin tower and moved the Melee weapon selection closer to the entrance, so that new players have a more immediate interaction with the movement system and combat. 
  * The first instance of combat in the Melee tutorial now happens much closer to where new players pick their Melee weapon. 
    * Also improved visual cues to communicate that combat has begun with the drop ship flying into the scene within the player's view, instead of at a distance in the previous version. 
  * Spaced the jump tutorial stages further apart to better explain single and double jumps. 
  * Shortened the path from the slide tutorial sequence to attacking an enemy to really make that moment feel heroic! 
  * Improved the overall combat sequence during the Secondary weapon tutorial with the new Ostron village arena that tightens engagement distance to the enemies, which was previously too far. 
  * Shortened the trek from the Ostron village to the bullet jumping tutorial significantly. 
  * Changed the bullet jumping tutorials to occur in areas that are more conducive to bullet jumping: 
    * The basic Bullet Jump tutorial now occurs in an area (with many beautiful waterfalls might I add) where you bullet jump from platform to platform. 
      * Within this area, we also added a new tutorial popup to explain how to Bullet Jump UP to reach platforms above you. 
    * The Bullet Jump + Slide tutorial area now occurs on a flat surface instead of slope. 
      * Previously, the Bullet Jump + Slide tutorial popup occurred in an uphill tunnel, which made it feel sluggish and didn’t properly communicate how beneficial it is. 
      * Now, in the updated area, players will get a proper feel for the momentum you get by accomplishing this set of movements. 
    * Added tutorial popup to teach players that Bullet Jumping repeatedly will help them move faster. 
  * Moved the “release the lockdown” console closer to the doors upon exiting the Captain Vor fight and made it a single button interaction instead of needing to hack it. 
  * Added more exploding barrels to the combat segment that follows acquiring a Primary weapon for a fun impact moment. 
  * Ship defense segment changes: 
    * Added Lotus transmission upon seeing the Liset Landing Craft to explain that it needs power and needs to be activated via the marked console. 
      * Previously, only the marker appeared without explanation as to what the player was doing by activating it before doing so. 
    * This segment now takes place in an entirely new tile with a layout that improves visibility of enemies and engagement distance. 
      * The previous tile took place on a platform with various surfaces of different elevations around it, which made combat feel distant and wasn’t conducive to players testing out all the new skills they just learned.

**Player Cold Status & Arctic Eximus Rework** 

In this update we adjusted Arctic Eximus enemies to make them less punishing and make it easier to engage in counter-play. This led to some changes in the way that Cold Status affects players. 

Player Cold Status Changes* 

**Before:** Getting afflicted with Cold Status would result in a 25% slowdown with no stacking for a 6 second duration. 

  * **Now:** Cold Status results in a 10% slowdown with an individual 6 second duration per stack, stacking up to 4 times for Players. (Players remain unable to be frozen solid) 



This change results in an overall potential of having up to 40% speed reduction but does not halt player movement completely. 

* These changes only apply to Cold Status against players (from sources such as environmental hazards and enemies), and does not alter how Cold Status affects enemies. 

**Arctic Eximus Changes** 

The Arctic Eximus has a unique application of the Cold Status effect. Under the previous system, the Arctic Eximus’ slowdown aura was invisible and much larger than their Globe suggested. Multiple Arctic Eximus auras could also overlap and apply their slowdown exponentially which made playing against them frustrating. To address this and to add more clarity to how their effects function we have made the following changes: 

  * Arctic Eximus enemies no longer have an invisible slowdown aura, instead the area of effect of their Cold Status is limited to the inside of their Globe. 
  * Arctic Eximus enemies now apply one stack of Cold Status per second if a player is inside their Globe. 
  * While inside of their Globe, Cold Status will stack up to 4 times for Players without duration. 
  * Cold Status from the Arctic Eximus is immediately removed if you leave the Globe, destroy the Globe, or kill the Eximus. 



We believe that these changes will make taking on these enemies more engaging and will also reduce the frustration that may arise from being held in place by status effects. As with any change we are open to your feedback on this rework.
